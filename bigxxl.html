Micropenis

A micropenis is a condition in which a male has a penis that is abnormally small in size. In general, a micropenis is defined as a penis that is less than 2.5 standard deviations below the average length for a newborn male. This means that a micropenis is rare, occurring in about 6% of male births. While a micropenis can vary in size, it is typically less than 3.5 inches (9 centimeters) in length when erect. This can cause significant distress and anxiety for affected individuals, as it can impact their sexual function, self-esteem, and overall quality of life. Various medical conditions can cause micropenis, including hormonal disorders, chromosomal abnormalities, and genetic conditions. Micropenis can also be a side effect of certain medications, such as steroids. It is important to note that having a micropenis does not necessarily mean that an individual will have difficulty with sexual function or fertility, as these factors can be influenced by various other factors and are not solely determined by penis size.

What causes micropenis?

  • Before birth, a male infant's genitalia develops in response to certain hormones, mainly androgens.

  • It's not always clear why some boys are born with a hormone disorder that causes micropenis. A family history of micropenis may raise the risk. A 2011 French study, suggests that fetal exposure to pesticides and other chemicals may increase the chances of micropenis development.
  • If his body doesn't produce enough androgens or if the body doesn't respond normally to androgen production, one result can be a micropenis, also called a microphallus.

  • While a micropenis can develop on its own, with no other hormone-related conditions, it can occur along with other disorders.

What it is and what isn't

Assuming there are no other health concerns, a micropenis functions the same as a normal, healthy penis. The ability to urinate and become erect shouldn't be affected.

A micropenis is sometimes associated with a lower sperm count, however, so fertility may be reduced.



Causes of Penis Shrinkage

Note that masturbation does not cause penis shrinkage by any means. The list below is the main causes.

  1. Aging: As you age, your penis may shrink as the collagen in the penis breaks down, resulting in less elasticity and less length.
  2. Smoking and Alcohol: Smoking has substances that diminish blood flow and can increase the risk of the narrowing of blood vessels. Alcohol can reduce testosterone, which can also lead to shrinkage.
  3. Obesity: Excess fatty tissue can inhibit the erection of the penis, resulting in shrinkage.
  4. Hypertension: High blood pressure can cause the arteries to weaken and constrict, limiting the blood flow to the penis and causing it to shrink.
  5. Peyronie's Disease: This condition is characterized by the formation of hard, fibrous tissue just beneath the skin of the penis. This tissue can cause the penis to bend or curve, resulting in a shorter and narrower shaft.
  6. Radiation Therapy: Radiation therapy may be used to treat prostate cancer, and it can damage the tissue of the penis and cause it to shrink.
  7. STIs: Some STIs like gonorrhea, syphilis, Chlamydia, and Genital herpes can also cause penis shrinkage in rare cases.
